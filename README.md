# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard

An end-to-end HR Analytics project built using **Power BI**, **Power Query**, **DAX**, and **Excel** to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, and key HR metrics. The project demonstrates how data can be transformed into actionable business insights through interactive dashboards.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The objective of this project is to analyze HR data and identify factors contributing to employee attrition. The dashboard helps HR professionals and business leaders monitor workforce trends, evaluate employee demographics, and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX
- Microsoft Excel

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains **1,470 employee records** with information such as:

- Employee Demographics
- Age
- Gender
- Department
- Education
- Job Role
- Job Satisfaction
- Attrition Status
- Years at Company
- Income
- Business Travel
- Performance Rating

---

## 📊 Dashboard Highlights

The dashboard includes:

- Overall Employees
- Active Employees
- Employee Attrition
- Attrition Rate
- Average Employee Age
- Department-wise Attrition
- Employee Distribution by Age Group
- Job Satisfaction by Role
- Education Field-wise Attrition
- Attrition Analysis by Gender and Age Group

Interactive slicers allow users to filter the dashboard by education level and explore the data dynamically.

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Total Employees: **1,470**
- Active Employees: **1,233**
- Attrition Count: **237**
- Attrition Rate: **16.12%**
- Average Employee Age: **37 Years**

### Business Insights

- Research & Development has the highest employee attrition.
- Employees aged **25–34** represent the largest workforce segment.
- Life Sciences employees show higher attrition compared to other education fields.
- Employee satisfaction varies across different job roles.
- The dashboard enables HR teams to identify high-risk employee groups and support retention strategies.

---

## 📈 Business Recommendations

- Improve employee engagement within high-attrition departments.
- Strengthen career development programs for employees aged 25–34.
- Monitor job satisfaction regularly to identify improvement opportunities.
- Implement targeted retention strategies for critical employee groups.
- Use dashboard insights to support workforce planning and HR decision-making.
